Medically Supervised Detoxification

Medical detox Miami services provide safe, comfortable withdrawal management for individuals physically dependent on alcohol, opioids, benzodiazepines, and other substances. Our alcohol detox Miami and drug detox Miami programs prioritize patient safety through continuous medical monitoring and symptom management protocols.

🚨 Critical Medical Supervision

Detoxification represents the critical first phase of addiction treatment, clearing harmful substances from the body while managing potentially dangerous withdrawal symptoms. Some withdrawals can be life-threatening without proper medical supervision.

Substance Withdrawal Timeline Dangerous Symptoms Medical Intervention
Alcohol 6 hours - 7 days Seizures, delirium tremens Anti-seizure medications, 24/7 monitoring
Opioids 12 hours - 10 days Severe discomfort, dehydration Medication-assisted treatment (MAT)
Benzodiazepines 1-4 weeks Seizures, psychosis Gradual tapering, medical supervision
Stimulants 3-14 days Depression, suicidal thoughts Psychological support, monitoring

Our medical detox team includes addiction medicine physicians, registered nurses, and mental health professionals specially trained in withdrawal management. They utilize FDA-approved medications to minimize discomfort, prevent medical complications, and reduce cravings that could lead to premature treatment dropout.

Frequently Asked Questions About Miami Addiction Treatment

What substances and addictions do you specialize in treating?

Our comprehensive treatment programs address the full spectrum of substance use disorders including alcohol use disorder, opioid addiction (prescription painkillers, heroin, fentanyl, synthetic opioids), stimulant addiction (cocaine, crack cocaine, methamphetamine), benzodiazepine dependence, marijuana use disorder, and prescription drug abuse involving multiple medication classes. We also have specialized expertise in treating polysubstance abuse where multiple substances are used simultaneously.

What is the typical duration of treatment programs?

Treatment length varies significantly based on individual factors including addiction severity, substance type, co-occurring mental health conditions, previous treatment history, and personal recovery goals. Residential inpatient programs typically range from 30 to 90 days, with 60-day programs being most common for comprehensive treatment. Many patients benefit from extended residential stays of 90 days or longer for severe, long-term addictions.

How does insurance coverage work for addiction treatment?

Most major insurance plans now provide comprehensive coverage for addiction treatment services due to mental health parity laws requiring equal coverage for mental health and substance use disorders. Coverage typically includes medical detoxification, inpatient rehabilitation, outpatient therapy, medication-assisted treatment, and aftercare services. We conduct detailed benefit verifications for every patient and provide written cost estimates before treatment begins.

What can I expect during the detoxification process?

Detoxification experiences vary dramatically depending on the substance(s) involved, duration and intensity of use, individual health status, and psychological factors. Our medical team provides 24/7 monitoring and uses FDA-approved medications to minimize discomfort and prevent complications. We also provide psychological support throughout detox to address anxiety, depression, and other emotional challenges that commonly occur during withdrawal.

How are families involved in the treatment process?

Family involvement is not only encouraged but considered essential for optimal treatment outcomes. Addiction affects entire family systems, not just the individual using substances, so healing must occur at the family level as well. We offer structured family therapy sessions, multi-family group sessions, family education workshops, and communication skills training. Family members learn about addiction as a medical condition, develop healthy boundaries, practice supportive communication techniques, and address their own trauma and stress.

What types of therapy and treatment approaches do you use?

We utilize evidence-based treatment modalities that have demonstrated effectiveness in clinical research and practice. These include Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), trauma-informed care, motivational interviewing, family therapy, group therapy, and medication-assisted treatment when appropriate. We also incorporate holistic approaches such as mindfulness training, art therapy, recreational therapy, and wellness programs.
